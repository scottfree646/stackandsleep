A white noise machine is the cheapest, simplest sleep upgrade you can make. A $49 device placed 1 metre from your bed can reduce sleep fragmentation more reliably than many $300 supplements. Here's what we found after testing seven of them.

## Why white noise works

Sound-induced microarousals are one of the most common causes of poor sleep quality in urban environments. You may not remember waking — but your sleep tracker will show the fragmentation. White noise masks the intermittent sounds that cause those arousals by providing a constant audio baseline.

It doesn't make you sleep more deeply. It just reduces how often you're pulled out of the sleep you'd otherwise have.

## The best options

### LectroFan Classic — best overall ($49)

The LectroFan Classic has been the benchmark for a reason. It produces 10 fan sounds and 10 variations of white, pink, and brown noise. The volume ceiling is genuinely high — high enough to mask most urban environments. The digital sound engine means no moving parts, no mechanical failure, no wearing out.

It doesn't have a timer, doesn't integrate with any app, and has no alarm function. It does exactly one thing: produce reliable, consistent sound. For most people, that's enough.

### Yogasleep Dohm Classic — best natural sound ($45)

The Dohm is a mechanical fan inside a plastic housing, making genuine airflow sound. For people who find electronic white noise artificial or grating, the Dohm's natural quality is immediately noticeable.

The trade-off: only one sound type, limited volume range compared to LectroFan, and the mechanical parts will eventually wear out (typically 3–5 years of nightly use).

### Hatch Restore 2 — best if you want a sleep routine system ($169)

The Hatch Restore 2 is the premium choice. It combines white noise with a large sound library, a sunrise alarm, a reading light, and a programmable sleep routine. The app integration is reliable.

At $169, it's only worth buying if you'll use the full system — the alarm, the light, the routines. If you want white noise only, LectroFan at $49 does it better for the purpose.

## What we'd skip

**Bose Sleepbuds II ($249)** — They work, but earbuds in bed are uncomfortable for most people and the battery requires nightly charging. Worth considering only if you share a bed and need to avoid disturbing a partner.

**Marpac WHISH ($79)** — Fine. But the LectroFan Evo does more for less.

## Our recommendation

For most people: **LectroFan Classic ($49)**. Buy it, put it on your bedside table or nightstand 1 metre from the bed, set it to a mid-volume white or pink noise, and leave it running all night.

If you want sunrise alarm integration and sleep routines: **Hatch Restore 2**.

If you prefer natural fan sound: **Yogasleep Dohm Classic**.
